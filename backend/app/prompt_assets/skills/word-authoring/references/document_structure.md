# Document Structure

Choose the structure by document intent. Keep headings specific to the user's
topic rather than copying these labels verbatim.

## Deep Research Report

1. Title page or title block.
2. Executive summary: 3-6 decisive findings and implications.
3. Research scope and method: sources, timeframe, definitions, limits.
4. Context and baseline: market, organization, policy, or project background.
5. Core findings: evidence-led sections with charts or tables.
6. Interpretation: why findings matter for the audience.
7. Options or recommendations: trade-offs, prerequisites, risk.
8. Implementation roadmap: owners, timeline, milestones, dependencies.
9. Appendix: evidence table, calculations, source list, assumptions.

## Business Analysis Report

1. Objective and management question.
2. KPI snapshot.
3. Performance drivers.
4. Segment or benchmark analysis.
5. Problems and root causes.
6. Action plan and expected impact.
7. Monitoring metrics.

## Policy or System Document

1. Purpose and scope.
2. Definitions.
3. Roles and responsibilities.
4. Process or rules.
5. Exceptions and escalation.
6. Records, forms, and audit requirements.
7. Effective date and revision history.

## Section Writing Pattern

Each substantive section should include:

- Claim or section thesis.
- Evidence or data.
- Interpretation.
- Implication for the user's objective.
- Transition to the next section.

## When To Add Appendices

Add appendices for large tables, methods, source catalogs, interview notes,
formula details, or material that supports credibility but interrupts reading.
