# Word Style Contract

## Typography And Hierarchy

- Use consistent heading levels; do not skip from H1 to H3.
- Keep Chinese body paragraphs concise, usually 2-5 sentences.
- Use numbered headings only when the document benefits from formal structure.
- Avoid excessive bolding; reserve emphasis for key findings, risks, and actions.

## Tables

- Introduce every table before it appears.
- Include table title, source note, unit, and time period.
- Keep tables page-width friendly; move very wide tables to appendix.
- Use summary rows and grouping when the source supports them.

## Figures And Charts

- Reference figures in body text before or immediately after they appear.
- Use captions that explain the takeaway, not only the chart type.
- Do not create charts from unsupported data.
- Keep chart labels readable in DOCX and PDF exports.

## Citations And Source Notes

- Use compact source notes when formal citation style is unspecified.
- Cite source section, page, table, or sheet when available.
- Separate source facts from the author's recommendation.
- If a statement is inferred, do not cite it as a direct fact.

## Pagination And Export

- Avoid orphan headings at page bottoms.
- Keep figure/table captions with their objects.
- Check that generated TOC, numbering, headers, and footers remain coherent.
- The final DOCX should open without missing fonts, broken images, or layout collapse.
