# Document Quality Bar

This bar defines what "good" means for generated documents. Chief, authoring
skills, and QA must all use it.

## P0 Requirements

- The document answers the user's actual question.
- Current-task uploads are read and reflected.
- Reference examples are style-only unless explicitly marked as evidence.
- Every major claim is grounded or labeled as an assumption.
- Required sections for the selected document type are present.
- Preview and downloadable file derive from the same content.

## P1 Requirements

- Executive summary contains conclusions, not just background.
- Section titles are specific and informative.
- Tables and figures have captions, units, and source notes.
- Recommendations follow evidence and constraints.
- Terminology is consistent across the whole document.
- Risks, limitations, and open questions are visible.

## P2 Polish

- Paragraphs have topic sentences.
- Dense details are moved to appendix.
- Repetition is removed.
- Voice matches audience and document type.
- Numbering, references, and attachments are consistent.

## Reject The Draft If

- It is a generic essay.
- It summarizes attachments without answering the user.
- It copies example content.
- It invents facts, references, organizations, or numbers.
- It lacks a usable plan, source basis, or QA trail.
