# Slide Writing Rules

## Titles

- Prefer conclusion-style titles that state the slide's answer.
- Use 8-22 Chinese characters when possible.
- Avoid "概述", "分析", "总结", "下一步" unless paired with a specific claim.
- Do not put multiple conclusions in one title.

## Bullets

- Use 3-5 bullets for normal slides; 6 is the upper limit.
- Keep each bullet under 25 Chinese characters when possible.
- Start bullets with the useful noun or verb; avoid throat-clearing.
- Do not paste report paragraphs into slide bodies.

## Metrics

- Use metric cards for 1-3 key numbers.
- Include unit, period, and source hint.
- Mark estimates and calculations explicitly.
- Avoid decorative numbers that do not support the slide message.

## Tables

- Use tables only when comparison matters.
- Keep columns few; move dense tables to appendix.
- Add a takeaway line before or after the table.

## Speaker Notes

- Notes may include evidence refs, caveats, and presenter talk track.
- Notes should not contain essential claims absent from the visible slide.
- Keep notes concise enough for presenter readiness.
