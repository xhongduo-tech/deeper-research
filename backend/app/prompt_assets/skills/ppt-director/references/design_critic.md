# Design Critic

Reject and revise a slide when any of these are true:

- The title is a generic label instead of a conclusion.
- More than one unrelated idea competes for attention.
- A slide has more than six bullet-level facts.
- The chosen visual does not clarify the core message.
- Visual styling does not match the selected `.pptd`.
- Template demo facts, titles, or industry examples leak into final content.
- A number appears without evidence, source, calculation, or assumption status.
- Preview and export would produce different page structures.

## Preferred Revision Actions

- Split dense slides.
- Promote the most important claim into a lead panel.
- Convert numeric comparisons into metric cards, tables, or charts.
- Move explanation, caveats, and sources into speaker notes.
- Use fewer, stronger bullets.
- Replace decorative visuals with evidence-bearing visuals.

## Deck-Level Critique

- Check that slide roles vary naturally.
- Check that the deck has a visible narrative arc.
- Remove duplicate slides that say the same thing differently.
- Make sure the final recommendation follows from prior evidence.
